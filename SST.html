Why people travelled to India in ancient times?

People travelled to India for various reasons:

1-Trade: India was a hub for trade, with its rich resources like spices, silk, and precious stones attracting merchants from regions such as the Middle East, Central Asia, and Southeast Asia.
2-Cultural Exchange: India was a center of learning and culture, especially in cities like Nalanda. Scholars, philosophers, and artists traveled to exchange knowledge and ideas.,br> 3-Religious Pilgrimage: India was the birthplace of major religions such as Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, attracting pilgrims seeking spiritual enlightenment or to visit sacred sites.
4-Scientific and Technological Advancements: India's advancements in mathematics, astronomy, and medicine drew scholars and explorers from other parts of the world.
